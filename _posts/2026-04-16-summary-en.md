## [Cybersecurity now resembles proof-of-work systems.](https://www.dbreunig.com/2026/04/14/cybersecurity-is-proof-of-work-now.html) ⭐️ 7.0/10

The article 'Cybersecurity is Proof of Work Now', published on April 14, 2026, draws a direct analogy between modern cybersecurity defenses and proof-of-work systems, arguing that securing software increasingly depends on heavy computational expenditure and AI-driven methods like LLM scanning. This perspective is significant as it frames cybersecurity as a resource-based arms race, similar to cryptocurrency mining, which could heighten economic barriers for defense, favor well-funded actors, and reflect broader trends of AI integration into security operations. Notable details include the article's reliance on analysis from the AI Security Institute (AISI), which found no diminishing returns in model performance even with token budgets up to 100M, and community skepticism about the institute's industry bias and the primitive nature of current LLM-based vulnerability scanning techniques.

hackernews · dbreunig · Apr 14, 18:08

**Background**: Proof-of-work is a concept that requires participants to expend computational effort to achieve a goal, such as validating transactions in blockchain networks like Bitcoin. Originally proposed by Moni Naor and Cynthia Dwork in 1993 to deter denial-of-service attacks and spam, it was later adapted by Adam Back's Hashcash for email protection. The analogy in cybersecurity suggests that defending systems now involves similar computational burdens, making security akin to a proof-of-work mechanism where success is tied to resource expenditure.
