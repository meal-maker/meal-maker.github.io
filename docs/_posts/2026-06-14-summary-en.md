## [Census Bureau Bans Noise Infusion, Sparking Privacy Debate](https://desfontain.es/blog/banning-noise.html) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The US Census Bureau has banned the use of noise infusion, a differential privacy technique, from all statistical products. This policy change removes a key privacy protection method designed to prevent re-identification of individuals from published data. This decision weakens privacy safeguards for census data, increasing the risk that individuals' information could be reconstructed from aggregated statistics. It undermines public trust and could lead to data being weaponized for tracking, targeting, or other harmful purposes. The order explicitly targets differential privacy and also impacts other techniques that involve randomness, stating that coarsening should always be preferred and suppression only as a last resort. Earlier research showed that 2010 census data without such noise could be used to reconstruct individual records with high accuracy.

hackernews · nl · Jun 13, 13:54

**Background**: Noise infusion is a privacy technique that adds carefully calibrated random noise to statistical data to prevent identification of individuals. Differential privacy is a mathematical framework that provides formal guarantees for such noise addition, allowing aggregate insights while limiting leakage about specific people. The Census Bureau had used these methods to protect respondent confidentiality. The ban removes these protections, potentially making it easier to re-identify individuals from published data.

## [Pancreatic Tumor Treatment Reveals Potential Cancer 'Master Switch'](https://economist.com/science-and-technology/2026/06/12/treating-pancreatic-tumours-may-have-revealed-cancers-master-switch) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Researchers treating pancreatic tumors may have uncovered a key weakness in KRAS-driven cancers, which account for about 20% of all tumors. This discovery targets a previously considered 'undruggable' protein, opening new avenues for targeted therapies. KRAS is the most frequently mutated oncogene, yet it was long considered undruggable due to its smooth surface and lack of drug-binding pockets. If confirmed, this breakthrough could lead to effective treatments for pancreatic and other hard-to-treat cancers that harbor KRAS mutations. The finding applies specifically to pancreatic cancers with KRAS mutations, a subset of the roughly 20% of all cancers driven by RAS mutations. The study referenced in the article is registered as clinical trial NCT06625320 on ClinicalTrials.gov.

hackernews · andsoitis · Jun 13, 13:34

**Background**: KRAS is a protein that acts as a molecular switch, controlling cell growth and division. When mutated, it gets stuck in the 'on' position, driving uncontrolled cancer growth. For decades, KRAS was considered 'undruggable' because its surface lacks deep pockets where drugs could bind easily. Recent advances have produced inhibitors for one specific mutation (KRAS G12C), but many other mutations remain untargetable.

## [Frame-Perfect UI Animation Critique Sparks Debate](https://tonsky.me/blog/every-frame-perfect/) ⭐️ 7.0/10

A blog post by Nikita Prokopov argues that modern UI animations on systems like macOS contain 'imperfect' frames that detract from user experience, and calls for frame-perfect precision in every intermediate state. This critique touches on a fundamental tension in UI design between mathematical correctness and perceptual acceptability, potentially influencing how developers and designers approach animation quality in operating systems and web interfaces. The article uses slowed-down video examples to highlight frames where UI elements appear in awkward positions or with mismatched transitions, such as the macOS save dialog and Notes app animations.

hackernews · ravenical · Jun 13, 11:40

**Background**: UI animation often follows principles derived from traditional animation, such as easing and arcs, to create natural motion. However, achieving frame-perfect quality requires precise timing and coordination between rendering and input events, which is challenging on variable refresh rate displays and multitasking environments. The human visual system is also forgiving of certain imperfections during motion, which some commenters argue makes the author's standard too strict.

## [Google repurposes retired phones for low-carbon computing](https://research.google/blog/a-low-carbon-computing-platform-from-your-retired-phones/) ⭐️ 6.0/10

Google Research has proposed using retired phone motherboards to build a low-carbon computing cluster, with plans to deploy a datacenter made from 2,000 Pixel smartphones for university researchers. This initiative could significantly reduce e-waste and carbon emissions from manufacturing new hardware, offering a sustainable, low-cost computing alternative that extends the lifecycle of old devices. The approach involves updating the operating system and disabling consumer protections like the low-memory killer daemon, but community comments highlight security risks from using outdated devices on networks.

hackernews · vikas-sharma · Jun 13, 09:38

**Background**: Phone cluster computing has been explored for years, such as the Ubispark project from Finland and the DroidCluster research, but widespread reuse is hindered by locked bootloaders and limited security update support from OEMs. Google's plan leverages its Pixel devices with longer support windows and aims to prove that old hardware can replace new servers in cloud computing.
