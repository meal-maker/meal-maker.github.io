## [Supreme Court Rules in Favor of Cox, Limiting ISP Liability for Copyright Infringement.](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/25/us/politics/supreme-court-cox-music-copyright.html) ⭐️ 8.0/10

On March 25, 2026, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled in Cox Communications v. Sony Music, holding that internet service providers are not directly liable for copyright infringement committed by their subscribers using peer-to-peer file sharing tools like BitTorrent. This decision significantly limits the legal exposure of ISPs under copyright law, potentially reducing their incentive to monitor user activity and impacting how copyright holders enforce their rights online, shaping future internet policy and enforcement strategies. The Court cited the 1984 Betamax case (Sony Corp. v. Universal City Studios) to emphasize that liability for infringement by others requires intent, and the ruling relates to the DMCA's safe harbor provisions which protect ISPs that cooperate with copyright holders and terminate repeat infringers.

hackernews · oj2828 · Mar 25, 15:02

**Background**: Internet service providers (ISPs) have been protected under the DMCA's safe harbor provisions, specifically the Online Copyright Infringement Liability Limitation Act, which shields them from liability if they take reasonable steps to address repeat infringers. Copyright infringement in music piracy often occurs via peer-to-peer file sharing protocols like BitTorrent, which allow decentralized sharing of files. The Betamax case established a precedent that technology providers are not liable for infringement by users unless they have specific intent to promote infringement.

## [ARC-AGI-3 is a new interactive benchmark for evaluating artificial general intelligence.](https://arcprize.org/arc-agi/3) ⭐️ 7.0/10

The ARC Prize Foundation launched ARC-AGI-3 on March 25, 2026, as an interactive reasoning benchmark with over 1,000 levels across 150+ environments that require AI agents to explore, learn, plan, and adapt without prior instruction. This benchmark is significant because it shifts AGI evaluation from static memorization tests to dynamic learning scenarios, providing a more accurate measure of human-like general intelligence and driving progress in AI research towards true adaptability. ARC-AGI-3 uses Relative Human Action Efficiency (RHAE) to score AI systems by comparing their per-level action efficiency to a human baseline defined as the second-best first-run human by action count, with the best AI in the preview phase scoring only 12.58% against a human baseline of 100%.

hackernews · lairv · Mar 25, 18:16

**Background**: The ARC-AGI series began with ARC-AGI 1 in 2019, which tested if models could learn a small skill from examples and apply it to novel cases to prevent memorization. ARC-AGI-3 is the first interactive benchmark in this series, designed to measure intelligence through real-time exploration and adaptation in unique environments.
