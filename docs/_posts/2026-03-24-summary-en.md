## [FCC updates covered list to include foreign-made consumer routers.](https://www.fcc.gov/document/fcc-updates-covered-list-include-foreign-made-consumer-routers) ⭐️ 7.0/10

The Federal Communications Commission (FCC) has updated its Covered List to include all consumer-grade routers produced in foreign countries by default, classifying them as posing an unacceptable risk to national security. Manufacturers must now seek Conditional Approval from the Department of War (DoW) or Department of Homeland Security (DHS) to obtain FCC equipment authorizations for such routers. This regulatory change could significantly impact the availability and cost of consumer routers in the U.S. market, as many popular brands are manufactured abroad. It underscores the U.S. government's intensified focus on securing telecommunications supply chains against potential foreign cyber threats and espionage. The update applies specifically to new models seeking FCC equipment authorization, while existing routers already on sale are not affected. Manufacturers can bypass the default ban by obtaining Conditional Approval, which involves demonstrating that their routers meet security standards determined by DoW or DHS.

hackernews · moonka · Mar 23, 21:28

**Background**: The FCC's Covered List is established under the Secure and Trusted Communications Networks Act of 2019, which requires the identification of communications equipment and services that pose national security risks. Routers are added to this list based on determinations from an interagency executive branch body, citing risks such as supply chain vulnerabilities that could disrupt critical infrastructure. This action is part of ongoing U.S. efforts to mitigate threats from foreign-made technology in sensitive sectors.

## [Two pilots killed in plane-ground vehicle collision at LaGuardia Airport.](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy01g522ww4o) ⭐️ 6.0/10

Two pilots died after a collision between an aircraft and a ground vehicle, specifically a fire truck, at LaGuardia Airport. The incident occurred when the fire truck was cleared to cross an active runway moments before the aircraft landed. This tragedy underscores critical safety gaps in airport surface operations and has ignited technical discussions on modernizing air traffic control through digitization and implementing automated collision-avoidance systems. It highlights the need for robust systems design in critical infrastructure to prevent human error and enhance overall aviation safety. Community comments indicate the fire truck was en route to another emergency and was initially cleared to cross the runway before ATC attempted to stop it. Technologies like Airport Surface Detection Equipment, Model X (ASDE-X) and Runway Status Lights (RWSL) exist to prevent such collisions but may not have been fully effective or deployed in this scenario.

hackernews · mememememememo · Mar 23, 07:24

**Background**: Airport surface safety often relies on systems like the Airport Surface Detection Equipment, Model X (ASDE-X), which provides air traffic controllers with detailed real-time data on vehicle and aircraft movements on runways. Runway Status Lights (RWSL) are automated visual alert systems embedded in runway pavements that use red LEDs to signal when it is unsafe to enter, cross, or take off. These technologies are part of ongoing efforts to digitize air traffic control and reduce runway incursions.
